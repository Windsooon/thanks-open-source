Why I build Thanks Open Source

I learned to program by myself at my 20s. I don't have any friends know programming since I major in civil engineering and I also suck at English. So most of the time I learned from outdated translated docs and low-quality blogs. And also I'm in debt for can’t a great job in programming, so I feel quite helpless at that time.
One day I meet a problem and don't know how to solve it after search online, I used Github issues to ask the maintainers for help (After that, I know I shouldn’t do that. Instead, I should use stackoverflow.com). After all, the maintainers are so kind, gentle to answer my silly questions. See

fadeIn() breaking appended transition and CSRF Failed when POST data using Alamofire

Here I specially thanks cnoon( The maintainer of Alamofire ) and desandro( The maintainer of masonry ), You guys make me a better programmer. I start to contribute to Django and also join some NGO programming camp to teach newbies. I learned a lot from my students, and I also enjoy the happiness teaching gives me that salary can never give me.
Most of the maintainers are excellent programmers, they can live an easy life without spent their spare time to build a project and help people they don't and will never know.
But they did, and year after years. And most of don't want to add the ad in their projects, so I build this project to say thank you and also let them have a place to recommend their side-projects or website.
In the end, I quote Feynman's words and edit a little bit.
"Programming is not the most important thing in the world, love is."
